What happens when a child loses their mother tongue?

They lose more than words. They lose stories, identity, connection to their ancestors, and the confidence to stand tall in their own skin.

Thanga changes that.

What is Thanga?

Thanga is a transformative linguistic theatre and music carnival for children aged 7-18.

Over immersive workshops, your child doesn't just learn isiZulu and SeSotho—they live it, breathe it, perform it.

  • Create wholly original performances from scratch
  • Master language comprehension and speaking
  • Unlock fearless creative confidence
  • Develop leadership that lasts a lifetime

Why Thanga?

This is not childcare. This is not edutainment.

Thanga is a once-in-a-lifetime transformative experience designed to help your child become the person the world isn't allowing them to be.

While other camps babysit, we build.

We centre your child's confidence, cultural identity, creative expression, and leadership skills.

The Stakes Are High

Every generation that loses its mother tongue loses a piece of its soul. Your child deserves to speak with the power of their ancestors, to move through the world with cultural confidence, and to create art that tells their story.
